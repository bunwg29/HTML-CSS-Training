Food for Thought

Good nutrition is a lifestyle, not a diet. IT starts with a new way of thinking about food. Think of your body as a vehicle that uses food for fuel. Fill your tank with the right type of fuel.

Do not approach your nutrition plan as a diet plan, but rather, a lifestyle change. If you make permanent change, you will see big results. The key to success is a daily focus on your goal.

Portion your meals and track your calories. There are many There mobile apps available to help track your activity level and calories.

Control impoluive urges to buy junk food, and instead, allow yourself to one "cheat" meal a week.

What to Eat

Use the following as a guideline:

  • Protein, such as eggs, chicken, and lean red meat
  • Vegetables, but avoid corn and peas
  • Fruit
  • Fat, such as olive oil, nute an seeds

Limit the consumption of bread, pasta, while potatoes, and processed food. These foods are high in carbodydrates.

When you shop for groceries, shop the outside aisles and buy organic when possible. Stock up on fresh vegetables, almond flour, and flax seed.

Meal of the Week

Merb-Roasted Chicken

Ingredients:

  • boneless, skinless chicken breaut (organic preferred)
  • 1 tup Merbes de Provence
  • 1 tup lemon juice
  • 1 tap olive oil
  • 2 tap sea salt
  • ¼ tap pepper

Instruction:

Combine all ingredients in a plastic bag and marinate for at least an hour, Preheat oven to 350 degroos. Bake chicken for 30-35 minutes or until juioes run clear. Broil on high for two minutes or until brown. Serve with a fresh mide salad.
